ğŸ Basic Rules of Cricket

Cricket is played between two teams of 11 players each. One team bats while the other bowls and fields. The batting team tries to score runs, and the bowling team tries to get them out. The team with the most runs wins the match.

  • Batting: Two players bat at a time and try to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets.
  • Bowling: Bowlers deliver six balls per over and aim to dismiss the batsmen.
  • Fielding: Fielders catch the ball, stop runs, and attempt run-outs.
  • Dismissals: A batsman can be out by bowled, caught, run-out, LBW, or stumped.
  • Match Types: T20 (20 overs), ODI (50 overs), and Test (5-day format).

ğŸ§  Cricket Terms You Should Know

  • Wicket: The set of stumps and bails the bowler aims to hit.
  • Over: A set of six legal deliveries by a bowler.
  • Boundary: 4 runs if the ball touches the ground before crossing the rope, 6 runs if it flies over.
  • Innings: The turn of a team to bat.
  • Umpire: The official who makes decisions during the match.
